About Us

A name that means new wings.

NayePankh — "new wings" — is the story of students who refused to wait for someone else to fix what they could see was broken.

Origin

It started with one bag of food.

When the Covid lockdown hit, basic necessities became out of reach for thousands of families overnight. A small group of students started cooking and distributing meals locally. What was meant to be a temporary relief effort never really stopped — because the need never really stopped either.

As the work grew, so did its scope: school supplies for children who'd lost a year of education, sanitary pads for young women too embarrassed to ask, clothes for families starting over with nothing. The name NayePankh stuck because it captured exactly what the work was — giving people the wings to lift themselves back up.

Our Journey

From relief effort to registered NGO.

Year One

Covid relief begins

A handful of students start distributing food to families and stray animals in their own neighbourhoods.

Year Two

The work expands

Clothing drives and school supply campaigns begin as more student volunteers join the cause.

Year Three

Official recognition

NayePankh is registered as a UP Government NGO and earns 12A and 80G certification, making donations tax-deductible.

Today

200,000+ lives touched

Now one of India's largest student-led organizations, featured across national media, still run entirely by students.

Our Mission

To provide direct, dignified access to food, education, healthcare and basic necessities to underprivileged communities — and to prove that young people can lead this change themselves, starting now rather than waiting for someday.

Our Vision

An India where no child's future is decided by their family's income — where every person has the food, education and dignity they need to find their own wings.
