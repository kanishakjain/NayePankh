Our Programs

Four ways we show up for our communities.

Every program is run hands-on by student volunteers — no middlemen, no overhead-heavy bureaucracy, just direct support reaching the people who need it.

Program 01

Food Distribution

What began as a Covid relief effort is now a regular rhythm: cooked meals and ration kits delivered to families who can't reliably put food on the table, alongside feeding drives for stray animals across our operating cities.

Volunteers cook, pack and personally hand-deliver each meal — keeping the work grounded in direct human contact, not just logistics.

Program 02

Education Access

We distribute school uniforms, books, and stationery to children whose families can't cover the cost of schooling — because a missed school year compounds into a missed future.

Where possible, volunteers also run informal tutoring sessions, helping kids catch up on lost learning time.

Program 03

Menstrual Health Awareness

Most young women still feel humiliated discussing periods in public. We run awareness sessions with women and youth about menstrual hygiene, and distribute free sanitary pads to break both the stigma and the access gap.

Program 04

Clothing Drives

We collect gently-used and new clothing through community drives, sorting and distributing it directly to families — especially ahead of winter, when warm clothing becomes a basic necessity rather than a comfort.

Questions

Frequently asked

How do I know my donation reaches people directly?

We're a registered NGO with 12A and 80G certification, and all distribution drives are run and documented by our own student volunteers — no third-party contractors involved.

Can I choose which program my donation supports?

Yes — when you reach out via our contact page, let us know if you'd like your contribution directed toward a specific program like education or menstrual health.

Do I need experience to volunteer?

No experience needed — most of our volunteers are students who started with zero NGO background. We'll plug you into a program that fits your time and interests.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes, NayePankh is 80G certified, meaning your donation qualifies for a tax deduction under Indian tax law.

Pick a program, lend a hand.

Every program runs on volunteer time and small donations — both make a direct, visible difference.
